## Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud Targeting a College Student

UPI fraud has become quite common in recent times, especially among students who are new to handling online payments. A typical example is when a first-year college student receives a message or call claiming to be from a trusted source, like a delivery service or a payment app.

In one such case, a student was trying to sell an old textbook online. He was contacted by a person who showed interest and said he would make the payment through UPI. The scammer then sent a link and asked the student to “accept” the payment by entering his UPI PIN. Without realizing, the student followed the instructions and entered his PIN, thinking he was receiving money.

However, instead of receiving money, the amount was deducted from his account. Within a few minutes, multiple transactions were made and a significant amount was lost. The scammer stopped responding immediately after that.

Such frauds usually target people who are not fully aware of how UPI systems work. The main consequence is financial loss, but it also creates stress and panic for the victim. This case shows how a small mistake, like sharing or entering a UPI PIN in the wrong place, can lead to serious problems.
